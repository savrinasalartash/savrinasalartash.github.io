About Me

Savrina is a masters student studying Bioinformatics at the Georgia Institute of Technology. Her heart lies in research and improving the quality of life for people worldwide through healthcare innovation.

More About Me


My seed of purpose was planted from a young age, when my heart was captivated by the field of healthcare and the transformative power of medical advancements. That thirst for knowledge and innovation grew into a flourishing desire to improve the quality of life for individuals across the globe. I believe that to instill real change, one needs to continuously push themselves beyond the boundaries of their comfort zone, which is exactly what I have done. I am on a lifelong quest to acquire the skills and expertise necessary to meet the ever-evolving demands of modern times.

Through countless hours of study, not only have I acquired thousands of hours of research experience, but I have also taught myself skills in programming and prototyping. I am embracing the intersection of healthcare and technology, as it has the potential to revolutionize the way we approach patient care and improve health outcomes worldwide. As I move into graduate school, I will expand my horizons further than ever before, exploring the intersection of technology and the life sciences first-hand.

My journey to achieve my goals has not been smooth sailing, but I tackle every challenge that comes my way, knowing that I will make a difference in the lives of others. Every obstacle has become an opportunity for growth, and every setback a chance to learn and adapt. I dream of a world where healthcare is accessible, transformative, and there are no bounds to quality of life. My story continues, and my dedication to healthcare and research will leave a mark on the world, one life at a time.
